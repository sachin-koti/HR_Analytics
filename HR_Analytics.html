Employee Turnover Prediction - HR Analytics¶

There are three important things covered:

1) Descriptive Analytics - What happened?
2) Predictive Analytics - What might happen?
3) Prescriptive Analytics - What action should be taken?

Problem:¶

One of the most common problems for a company is turnover.

In the context of human resources, turnover is the act of replacing an employee with a new employee. Few of the reasons could be resignations, terminations, transfers, retirements, deaths, etc.

As per the Center of American Progress, replacing a worker earning about 50,000 dollars cost the company about 10,000 dollars or 20% of that worker's yearly income.

Cost includes: (There could be many factors that add to turnover cost.Few of the important ones are listed below)

  • Off-boarding cost
  • Hiring costs (advertising, interviewing, hiring)
  • Cost of onboarding a new person (training, management time)
  • Cost of Overtime payments to employees who cover the left employee's work.
  • Lost productivity (a new employee usually takes 1-2 years to reach the productivity of an existing employee)

Annual Cost of Turnover = (hiring + onboarding + unfilled time + other costs) x (# Employees x Annual Turnover Percentage)

Annual Cost of Turnover = (1000 + 500) x (15,000 x 24%)

Annual Cost of Turnover = 5400000

Example¶

  1. Jobs(earning under 30k a year) - the cost to replace a 10 dollar/hour retail employee would be ~3300 dollars.
  2. Jobs(earning a 30k-50k a year) - the cost to replace a 40k manager would be ~8000 dollars.
  3. Jobs of executives (earning 100k+ a year) - the cost to replace a 100k CEO is ~213,000 dollars.

Problem Definition:¶

To design a predictive model based on the historic employee turnover data for estimating the probability whether an employee turnovers or not and accordingly design a system of actions to be taken to reduce the turnover rate and improve the workforce retention. Hence, significantly reducing the high costs incurred on the organization.

Recall or Precision?¶

It depends on how much cost/weight one bears on the two types of error: 1)False Positives or 2)False Negatives

What's the cost of having a FN or a FP?

Optimize Recall when:

 1.You want to limit False Negatives.
 2.FN > FP
 3.e.g: Predicting Cancer Patients or Credit Card Frauds

Optimize Precision when:

1.You want to limit False Positives.
2.FP > FN
3.Spam vs Not Spam

I think in this case of Turnover prediction modeling, it's better to go with optimizing Recall since the cost of bearing a FN is much higher than that of FP.

If our model results in a FN:

1.Then we will eventually miss out on that employee. 
2.After he/she resigns the job, to replace him/her, it takes a whole lot of time, efforts and money to recruit a new candidate for that role and conduct the onboarding, orientation, KT, etc.. which accounts to extra costs for the company.

3.This cost is more than the cost of early identification of employees who may potentially turnover.Once we get an approximate probability of who may plan to leave the company,the company can try to convince them,atleast few of them to stay by promising and providing satisfactory perks or project change or promotion,etc.Hence avoiding huge costs incurred otherwise.

Prescriptive Analytics¶

Retention Plan¶

We cannot solely depend on the output of our model since it is being used for people.Instead, we can use its probability output and design our own system to treat each employee accordingly.

1.Safe Zone (Green)  - This zone employees are considered safe.
2.Low Risk Zone (Yellow) - Should be taken into consideration.May become potential turnover eventually. 
3.Medium Risk Zone (Orange) - These employees are at risk of turnover. Should be monitored and taken care accordingly.
4.High Risk Zone (Red) - These employees are at the highest peak of turnover.Should be acted upon immediately.

What to Optimize¶

We want our ML model to catch as much as possible potential turnovers. Our target is to catch all of the higly probable turnover employees at the cost of flagging some low-risk non-turnover employee.(i.e in ML terms, limit the False Negatives even if it happens at the cost of False Positives).

Solution 1:¶

  • Ranking the employees by their probability of leaving, then allocating a limited incentive budget to the highest probable turnover instances.
  • Or we can allocate the incentive budget to those employees which can incur highest loss if turnovered.This can be done with high-salary executives/employees, high or mid-level managers/team leads or senior team members.

Solution 2:¶

  • Developing learning programs for potential turnover employees.Then monitoring their performance and measure progress.
  • Thanksgiving for team members' success.
  • Helping team with career development.

Requiring Special Focus:¶

  1. When we talk about 'Turnover' feature, we don't know if the employees that left are interns, contractors, full-time or part-time.These are important aspects to be considered when building a predictive modeling solution since weights associated with the turnover costs for each type of employee varies accordingly. A high-salary employee would need a costlier form of treatment.
  2. We may not be sure about the 'Evaluation' feature since evaluation is highly subjective, and can vary greatly based on who is the evaluator. If the employee and the evaluator knows each other, then he/she will mostly have a higher score.

Final Thoughts:¶

Though low-turnover rate is the goal for most organizations, the benchmark for turnover rate varies accordingly depending upon the industry-type, type of job, company size, region, etc. Organizations have to consider the individual as well as industry-related factors into account as they decide their target turnover rate, learn reasons for turnover, and make changes to reduce the employee turnover rate and improve their workforce retention.

Note: This is a generic model built on a dataset provided by the Kaggle website. When one plans to design their own turnover prediction model, this project would be used as a reference template to start afresh. This project serves no real benefit unless modified or adjusted as per the requirements and factors affecting turnover of the organization(s) under study.
